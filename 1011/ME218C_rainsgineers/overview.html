Design Overview

Goal: Design and build a wirelessly-controlled seafaring robot capable of playing a king-of-the-hill inspired game.

Project Motivation

The stealth house boat is Team Rainsgineers’ final project for Stanford’s ME 218C: Mechatronics. The goal of the project was to build a remote controlled boat capable of playing a competitive game of king of the hill. Boats competed in teams of two to capture various atolls spread out in a Stanford fountain.

Key Project Challenges

  • Develop a creative control system for the boat utilizing at least three sensing modalities as inputs.
  • Implement wireless communications to control boat movement, establish teammates, and capture atolls.
  • Develop a class-wide communications protocol to standardize the myriad of communications taking place at once.
  • Develop the circuits and software to read the RFID cards attached to atolls, decode them, and send them to atolls in order to capture them.
  • Design an integrated system consisting of multiple processors, H-Bridges, and transceivers, and communicating between the various components using UART, SPI, and RF protocols.
  • Designing a vessel with a robust drivetrain and adequate waterproofing to protect the circuits, batteries, and mechanisms on board.
